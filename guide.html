✨ Firefly — Keyword Highlighter

Automatically mark your keywords on every webpage you visit.

Installation

  1. Open Chrome and visit chrome://extensions.
  2. Toggle Developer mode on (top-right corner).
  3. Click Load unpacked and select the Firefly folder.
  4. Firefly's icon appears in your toolbar. Pin it for quick access.

Adding Keywords

  1. Click the Firefly icon in your toolbar.
  2. Type one keyword per line in the textarea.
  3. Pick a default color with the Color picker (optional — defaults to yellow).
  4. Click Add Keywords. Duplicates are skipped automatically.
Keywords are stored in Chrome sync storage, so they follow you across devices signed in to the same Chrome profile.

Per-Keyword Styling

Bold B

Each keyword has a small B button. Click it to toggle whether matches are bolded. Active = blue, inactive = gray.

Color and Highlight

Click the small colored swatch next to a keyword to open the color side panel. The popup expands to show:

  • Highlight color — pick any color.
  • No highlight checkbox — disables the background highlight (useful if you only want the keyword bolded).

Press Apply to save, or ✕ Close to discard.

On / Off Toggle

The On / Off button at the top of the list hides or restores all highlights everywhere without erasing your keyword list. Useful when a page becomes too visually busy.

Dynamic Pages

Firefly watches the page for new content as it loads (infinite scroll, single-page apps, async-rendered widgets). New text that matches your keywords is highlighted automatically after a brief delay.

If a site never seems to highlight anything, it may put text inside <input> or <textarea> elements, which Firefly intentionally skips to avoid breaking form fields.

Clearing Everything

Press Clear all in the popup to remove every keyword. The action is immediate and applies to all open tabs.
